Evidence-based 10-question survey for screening about the social determinants of health in the outpatient setting. From Garg A, et al. "Improving the management of family psychosocial problems at low-income children's well-child care visits: The WE CARE Project." Pediatrics 120, 547-558 (2007).

Evidence-Based Questions

Evidence-based screening questions you can use to ask your patients about a variety of specific topics.

  • TWO-QUESTION survey from Hager, et al. 2010

    • Within the past 12 months did you worry whether your food would
      run out before you got money to buy more?

    • Within the past 12 months, was there a time when the food you
      bought didn't last and you didn't have money to buy more?

    • For more information, check out the publication:
      http://pediatrics.aappublications.org/content/126/1/e26.full.html

    • See the document below

  • Do you need help in getting food by the end of the month? IF YES,
    would you like help with this (WE CARE)?

  • Do you have enough money to make ends meet (I-HELP)?
  • Do you have enough food to feed your family (I-HELP)?
  • Do you owe any money on your rent or utilities (I-HELP)?
  • Do you have affordable childcare (I-HELP)?
  • Do you have health insurance (I-HELP)?
  • Do you have a high school degree? IF NO – would you like help to get
    a GED (WE CARE)?
  • Do you have a job? IF NO – would you like help finding a job (WE
    CARE)?

  • TWO-QUESTION Survey (National Survey of American Families (1999)
    questions):

    • During the last 12 months, was there a time when (you/you and
      your family) were not able to pay your mortgage, rent or utility
      bills?

    • During the last 12 months, did you or your children move in with
      other people even for a little while because you could not
      afford to pay your mortgage, rent or utility bills?

  • Do you think you are at risk of becoming homeless? IF YES – would
    you like help with this (We Care)?

  • Like many of my patients who are trying to do the best they can for
    their family,

    • Are you concerned about the safety or stability of your housing
      (I-HELP)?

    • Is it hard to keep rodents, insects or mold away (I-HELP)?

    • Have you gotten behind on any rent or utility bills (I-HELP)?

    • Do you participate in any programs to reduce utility bills
      (I-HELP)?

  • Do you need daycare for your child? IF YES – would you like help
    finding it (We Care)?

  • Are you concerned about your child’s learning, performance, or
    behavior in school (I-HELP)?

  • If you have any of these concerns, have you asked school to conduct
    a special education evaluation? (If no, then request
    one immediately.) (I-HELP)

  • If your child has qualified for special education or reasonable
    accommodations, are you satisfied with the special help or
    accommodations your child receives (I-HELP)?

  • If your child has a medical problem, is the school providing
    necessary support (I-HELP)?

  • Are you concerned about discipline or safety issues at school
    (I-HELP)?
  • Are you feeling sad or hopeless a lot of the time? IF YES – would
    you like help with this (WE CARE)?

  • Many of my patients have experienced domestic violence or
    controlling behavior by a family member. Do you ever feel unsafe at
    home (I-HELP)?

  • Does your partner hit or verbally abuse you? IF YES – would you like
    help (WE CARE)?

  • Have you had difficulty accessing income supports or housing
    benefits because of birth certificate documentation or custodial
    status (I-HELP)?

  • Are you currently receiving child support (I-HELP)?

  • Are you concerned that a family member cannot take care of him- or
    herself due to illness or disability (I-HELP)?

  • Have you identified someone who will make decisions about your
    health care in the event that you become too ill or hurt to express
    your wishes (I-HELP)?

Care For The Uninsured So your patient does not have health insurance... there are still options for your patient to get care in Houston! Options include Federally Qualified Health Centers, free or charity clinics, the Harris Health System (for residents of Harris County) and more!

What are Federally qualified health centers (FQHCs)?

Federally qualified health centers (FQHCs) must serve an underserved area or population, offer a sliding fee scale, provide comprehensive services, have an ongoing quality assurance program, and have a governing board of directors.

Find a FQHC that is convenient for your patient at the HRSA website (search by zip code):

http://findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov

Eligibility

Women ages 18 to 44 who have little or no money. Maximum monthly income limits (see: maximum monthly limits).

What it offers:

  • A family planning exam

  • Screenings for diabetes, sexually transmitted diseases, high blood
    pressure, and breast and cervical cancers

  • Treatment of certain sexually transmitted diseases

  • Family planning counseling and education

  • Birth control, except emergency contraception

Must be 18 or older, a U.S. citizen or legal immigrant, don’t qualify for full Medicaid, CHIP, or Medicare Part A or B.

Patient navigators help people access health care at the appropriate level. If you don’t have time to help your patient find access to care or a health home – call a navigator who has the time and resources to help!

Navigators will:

  • Educate people about options of healthcare access

  • Assist people to identify a medical home

  • Assist people to obtain social services and other community resources

  • Educate people on the value of a medical home (Health Home), and assist in the development of family preventive health care plans

  • Contact a Patient Navigator to link your patients with access to care: call 713-783-4616!

Application Assistance

Free, region-wide, hotline for residents to call and receive advice and health information!

  • Available 24/7

  • Staffed by Registered Nurses and Community Health Workers

  • Upon calling residents can receive help with general health information, care advice, and determining the appropriate time to seek care

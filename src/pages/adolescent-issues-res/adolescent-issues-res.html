Adolescent Issues Information

  • In 2012, Harris County reported a teen birth rate of 42.7 births per 1,000 for 15-19 year olds1. This is compared to 44.4 births per 1,000 for 15-19 year olds in all of Texas, and 29.4 for the US teen birth rate2.

  • Based on 2012 US survey data, 6.6% of adolescents 12-17 years of age had smoked cigarettes in the past month and 12.9% had used alcohol in the past month3.

  • In a 2013 survey of Houston ISD high school students, 21.5% of students reported not having exercised once for at least 60 minutes in the week leading up to the survey4.

  • In a 2013 survey of Houston ISD high school students4:

    • 43.6% had ever used marijuana

    • 17.4% had ever used prescription drugs without prescriptions

    • 9.4% had ever used ecstasy

    • 6.6% had ever used methamphetamines

    • 5.9% had ever used heroin

    • 11.2% had ever used cocaine

  • In a 2013 survey of Houston ISD high school students, 31.4% of youth reported being sexually active, with 24.2% reporting not using any method to prevent pregnancy4.
